# Visual Modes — 12 Production Modes

> The full mode catalog. Modes 1-8 are documented in detail in [`system/visual-framework.md`](../../system/visual-framework.md). Modes 9-12 are added here.

---

## The 12 modes

| # | Mode | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Cinematic Dark | Founder takes, contrarian, premium positioning |
| 2 | Editorial Minimal | Educational, thought leadership, B2B |
| 3 | Futuristic Interface | AI / SaaS launches, technical breakdowns |
| 4 | Surreal Product Metaphor | Product-led, before/after, storytelling |
| 5 | Premium Founder/Operator | Founder narratives, build-in-public |
| 6 | Technical Blueprint | Architecture, technical breakdowns |
| 7 | Exploded Diagram | Teardowns, "how X works" |
| 8 | Meme-but-Premium | Cultural moments, viral attempts |
| 9 | Luxury Object | Premium products, hero shots |
| 10 | Abstract System | Frameworks, mental models, conceptual posts |
| 11 | AI Command Center | Agent / workflow / orchestration content |
| 12 | Before/After Split Scene | Transformations, comparisons, case studies |

For modes 1-8, see `system/visual-framework.md` for full composition, lighting, typography, and risks.

---

## Mode 9 — Luxury Object

**When to use:** when the product (physical or digital) deserves to feel like an artifact. Premium hero shots. Editorial product photography.

**Composition:**
- Single product object, centered or rule-of-thirds.
- Velvet, marble, brushed metal, leather, or matte plaster surface beneath.
- Soft single key light at 45°.
- Generous negative space; the object breathes.

**Image prompt style:**
> "[OBJECT] resting on a [SURFACE], shot in editorial product photography style, single soft key light from upper-right at 45°, soft shadow falling left, generous negative space at the bottom, hyperreal material detail, museum-quality render, no real brand logos, no text, leave the bottom-third empty for headline."

**Typography:** editorial serif, large, set in negative space.

**Risks to avoid:**
- Over-styled — too much smoke, glitter, sparkle.
- Centered-with-symmetry that reads as catalog.

## Mode 10 — Abstract System

**When to use:** when the post is about a *concept* or *system*, not an object. Frameworks, mental models, principles.

**Composition:**
- Abstract geometric forms (spheres, cubes, lattices, threads).
- 3D-rendered or paper-cut feel.
- Single accent color among neutrals.
- Composition implies relationship: connected nodes, layered planes, scale shifts.

**Image prompt style:**
> "An abstract 3D scene of [GEOMETRIC FORMS arranged to suggest STRUCTURE/RELATIONSHIP], soft daylight from upper-left, neutral base palette with one [ACCENT COLOR] highlight, hyperreal material detail (matte plaster, glass, brushed metal), depth-of-field falling off behind the central form, no text, no logos, leave the bottom-third empty for headline."

**Typography:** geometric sans, mid-size, restrained.

**Risks to avoid:**
- "Generic gradient blobs" trap.
- Abstract that doesn't connect to the post's argument.

## Mode 11 — AI Command Center

**When to use:** posts about AI agents, orchestration, multi-tool workflows, autonomous systems.

**Composition:**
- A holographic interface or floating control surface.
- Subtle glow, restrained data visualization.
- Charcoal or near-black base.
- Bloom and chromatic aberration kept *subtle*.
- One element pulses or glows in accent color.

**Image prompt style:**
> "A floating holographic control surface displaying [ABSTRACT WORKFLOW: nodes, agents, data flows], soft edge glow in [ACCENT_COLOR], deep charcoal background with subtle dust motes, atmospheric perspective, depth of field falling off behind the interface, ultra-detailed cinematic 3D render, no real product logos, no readable text on the interface — keep UI elements abstract and shape-driven, leave the bottom-third empty for typography."

**Typography:** geometric sans, accent for emphasis on one word.

**Risks to avoid:**
- "Iron Man HUD" cliché — too many circles and dial readouts.
- Fake screenshots that look like real apps.

## Mode 12 — Before/After Split Scene

**When to use:** transformations, comparisons, case studies. Particularly powerful when the after is dramatic.

**Composition:**
- Single canvas split vertically (or horizontally) into two halves.
- Same subject, two states.
- Lighting the same on both halves; only the *state* changes.
- Or: same scene, different camera angle implying time has passed.

**Image prompt style:**
> "A single composition split vertically: on the left, [BEFORE STATE]; on the right, [AFTER STATE]. Both halves share lighting from the upper-left at ~3200K, atmospheric haze. Hyperreal material detail. The split is implied (no hard line) by a soft transition through atmospheric depth. Bottom-third intentionally empty for headline. No text, no logos, no flat illustration."

**Typography:** sentence case serif, anchored bottom-center over the split.

**Risks to avoid:**
- Hard split-line that reads as collage.
- "Stock photography before/after" energy (cluttered desk → tidy desk = cliché).

## Choosing among modes 9-12

| Brief signal | Mode |
|---|---|
| Hero product shot, premium category | 9 |
| Conceptual / framework post | 10 |
| AI workflow / agent / orchestration topic | 11 |
| Transformation / comparison post | 12 |
| When unsure | 1 (Cinematic Dark) or 2 (Editorial Minimal) |

---

## Mode mixing

Default: one mode per carousel.

Allowed: hero on slide 1 in Mode 4 or 9 (high drama), then Mode 1 / 2 / 6 for slides 2-N (sustain). Always grade lighting and palette to match.

Never mix more than 2 modes in one carousel.
