# Authority Campaign Template (30 days)

> A focused 30-day push on a single topic to compound the brand's expertise. The audience leaves the campaign convinced this brand owns the topic.

---

## Arc

```
Week 1 — Foundation (4-5 posts): the brand's core arguments on the topic.
Week 2 — Mechanism (4-5 posts): why the arguments are true.
Week 3 — Application (4-5 posts): how to apply the arguments on Monday.
Week 4 — Proof + ask (4-5 posts): case studies, founder essays, then 1-2 CTA posts.
```

---

## Per-post structure

Same as `launch-campaign-template.md`. Format / angle / hook / body summary / CTA / platform / proof / notes.

---

## Worked example skeleton (Lumen, "interrupt-budget thesis" 30-day)

### Week 1 — Foundation
- Day 1: "Most senior operators are fighting the wrong battle." (Contrarian)
- Day 3: "The default looks like productivity. It's interruption with a friendly face." (Founder Perspective)
- Day 5: "What 'deep work' actually means in 2026." (Reframe)
- Day 7: Founder essay — Mira on the post-chat era. (Long form)

### Week 2 — Mechanism
- Day 8: "Why chat-shaped AI fails for focus, mechanically." (Technical Breakdown)
- Day 10: "Senior operators have fewer surfaces. Here's why." (Mechanism)
- Day 12: "The compound math of context-switching." (Benchmark)
- Day 14: Founder essay — Theo on the no-chat design decision. (Build in Public)

### Week 3 — Application
- Day 15: "What I removed first." (Workflow Reveal)
- Day 17: "Three categories of AI tools to kill this week." (Stop Doing X)
- Day 19: "How to run your morning without making decisions all day." (Hidden Feature)
- Day 21: "The 4 questions before installing any new tool." (Checklist)

### Week 4 — Proof + ask
- Day 22: Anonymized case (Before/After).
- Day 24: Benchmark drop with methodology. (Benchmark)
- Day 26: Founder reflection — what 30 days of writing taught us. (Founder Perspective)
- Day 28: Soft ask — invite to read the full essay collection.
- Day 30: Direct ask — try Lumen if the thesis resonates.

---

## Cadence rules

- 4-5 posts/week.
- Don't post every day; leave whitespace.
- Founder voice on at least 4 posts (one per week).
- Proof drop in week 4 — earlier drops feel premature.
- The CTA in weeks 1-3 is *follow / save / comment*, not *try*. The *try* CTA appears in week 4.

---

## What an authority campaign earns

- Audience growth on the channel.
- "I came for the [topic] essays" comments.
- DMs from peers and operators.
- A vocabulary the audience adopts ("interrupt budget," "structural not behavioral").
- A reference body of work the brand can keep linking back to.

---

## What an authority campaign does NOT earn

- Direct conversions during weeks 1-3. Conversion is week 4 only.
- Reach at the level of a viral / contrarian post. Authority compounds over weeks, not posts.

If the brief expects week-1 conversions, switch to a launch campaign.
