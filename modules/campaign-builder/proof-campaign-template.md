# Proof Campaign Template (7 days)

> A focused 7-day campaign that turns one real customer story (or one benchmark) into a multi-post deliverable. Compounds trust, surfaces decision-stage audiences.

---

## Arc

```
Day 1 — Setup (1 post): name the segment / problem the proof addresses.
Day 2 — Pressure (1 post): show the cost of the default.
Day 3 — Reveal (1 post): introduce the case / benchmark headline.
Day 4 — Mechanism (1 post): explain why the result happened.
Day 5 — Detail (1 post): segment breakdown, methodology, or context.
Day 6 — Implication (1 post): what this means for the audience.
Day 7 — Ask (1 post): the full case / data + CTA.
```

7 posts in 7 days. Compressed; high-density.

---

## Worked example skeleton (Lumen, "VP-Engineering case study")

### Day 1 — Setup
- Hook: "Most VPs of Engineering have stopped scheduling deep work. Here's one who got it back."
- Format: carousel
- Angle: Audience callout
- CTA: save

### Day 2 — Pressure
- Hook: "What 22 hours of meetings per week actually cost a senior engineering leader."
- Format: reel (30s)
- Angle: Visible cost
- CTA: comment

### Day 3 — Reveal
- Hook: "VP of Engineering at a Series-C devtools company: the brief that survived the day."
- Format: carousel (Before/After)
- Angle: Before/After
- Body summary: anonymized case from `proof-assets.md` — "the brief stopped me from re-deciding the day at every Slack ping."
- CTA: save

### Day 4 — Mechanism
- Hook: "Why the morning-brief beat the calendar tetris."
- Format: carousel
- Angle: Mechanism
- CTA: save

### Day 5 — Detail
- Hook: "The first 30 days, week by week."
- Format: carousel (Workflow Reveal)
- Angle: Workflow Reveal
- CTA: save / DM

### Day 6 — Implication
- Hook: "What this means if your week looks like hers."
- Format: long-form post
- Angle: Application
- CTA: DM

### Day 7 — Ask
- Hook: "Full case + the morning-brief anatomy."
- Format: carousel + linked long-form essay
- Angle: Founder Perspective + CTA
- CTA: try (or DM for early access if it's a waitlist proof campaign)

---

## Cadence rules

- 1 post/day for 7 days. Don't drop a day.
- Don't repeat the proof headline across all 7 posts — vary the angle each day.
- The first comment on each post links to the next post in the series (when the platform allows linking).
- Founder voice on at least 2 of the 7 posts.

---

## Required proof inventory

Before generating, confirm:

- The case is real and citable (per `proof-assets.md`).
- Permission level (`public` / `anonymized` / `not citable`).
- Verbatim quote (do not paraphrase).
- Methodology / sample size if it's a benchmark.
- Any compliance flags from `constraints.md`.

If proof is incomplete, surface gaps as `[MISSING_INPUT]` and pause the campaign.

---

## When NOT to run a proof campaign

- The proof is too thin for 7 posts (better as a single post).
- The customer hasn't agreed to public attribution and you can't anonymize.
- The numbers can't survive scrutiny (risk-of-claim violation).
- The campaign lands during a quiet period for the audience (e.g. mid-summer, between holidays).

A weak proof campaign damages trust more than no proof campaign.
