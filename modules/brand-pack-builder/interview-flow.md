# Interview Flow

> The 10-section interview the Brand Pack Builder runs in interview mode. One section at a time, with sharpening follow-ups when answers are vague.

---

## Section 1 — Brand basics

1. What's the brand name?
2. One line, plain language, no marketing speak — what is it?
3. Tagline? (optional)
4. Category — what would a stranger search for it under?
5. Stage — pre-launch / early-traction / scaling / established?
6. URL? (for human reference; do not visit)

**Sharpener:** if the one-liner contains "innovative," "platform," "solution," or "transform," ask for a plainer version.

---

## Section 2 — Audience

1. Who is the primary audience? (role + situation, not demographics)
2. What's their #1 pain in your category — in their words?
3. What do they want they can't currently get?
4. Who is explicitly NOT the audience?
5. Where do they hang out online? (specific places, not "LinkedIn")
6. What do they read / watch / listen to?

**Sharpener:** if the answer is "everyone" or "anyone who cares about X," push for a role + situation.

---

## Section 3 — Offer

1. What does the brand sell, in plain language?
2. The job to be done, from the customer's perspective?
3. The unique mechanism — *why* does it work?
4. What does it explicitly *not* do?
5. Pricing model? Public tiers (if any)?
6. Delivery model? (self-serve, hybrid, white-glove)

**Sharpener:** if no mechanism, push: "If a customer asks 'why does this work?', what's the answer?"

---

## Section 4 — Positioning & differentiation

1. Positioning statement: "[BRAND] is the [CATEGORY] for [AUDIENCE] who want [OUTCOME] without [PAIN]."
2. Top 3 differentiators (defensible, specific).
3. Top 3 alternatives the audience considers (including "do nothing").
4. Where do you win against each? Where do they win?

**Sharpener:** if differentiators include "we care about customers," push for something measurable.

---

## Section 5 — Tone of voice

1. Voice in 5-7 adjectives.
2. Reference brands whose voice you admire (without copying)?
3. Words / phrases you love?
4. Words / phrases you ban?
5. Formal or casual? Lowercase-friendly?
6. First-person ("I"), plural ("we"), or third-person?
7. Emoji policy?

**Sharpener:** if adjectives include "friendly" or "professional," push for something more specific (e.g. "deadpan," "operator-grade," "warm-but-uncompromising").

---

## Section 6 — Visual identity

1. Primary brand colors (hex)?
2. Accent color?
3. Typography (headline + body)?
4. Visual mode preference — pick one from `system/visual-framework.md`?
5. Logo placement preference?
6. Mood references — films, magazines, brands you admire visually?

**Sharpener:** if "I don't know yet" → recommend a mode based on the brand voice and audience.

---

## Section 7 — Proof assets

1. Best customer story you can share publicly?
2. Verbatim testimonials? (paste them)
3. Hard numbers (revenue, users, retention, time-saved) — citable publicly?
4. Awards, press, public artifacts?
5. Things you have but CAN'T cite publicly?

**Sharpener:** for every public-citable claim, ask "what's the source / methodology / sample size?"

---

## Section 8 — Claims & constraints

1. Claims you ARE comfortable making publicly?
2. Claims you are NOT comfortable making (legal, factual, ethical)?
3. Compliance posture? (HIPAA / GDPR / SEC / FDA / SOC 2 / none)
4. Trigger phrases requiring legal review?
5. Competitors off-limits to name?
6. Image / visual constraints? (faces, real screenshots, etc.)

**Sharpener:** if no constraints listed, ask: "If you had to add one constraint to protect the brand, what would it be?"

---

## Section 9 — Content examples

1. 1-3 pieces of YOUR existing content that worked — what was the topic, format, outcome?
2. 1-2 that didn't land — what went wrong?
3. Content patterns banned internally?
4. Content rituals you've kept (e.g. weekly recurring series)?

**Sharpener:** for each "worked" example, ask "what specifically about it worked?"

---

## Section 10 — CTA preferences

1. Default CTA goal? (save / comment / click / DM / follow / try)
2. Preferred CTA phrasing?
3. CTAs you ban?
4. The long-term business action the carousels eventually point to?

**Sharpener:** if multiple CTAs are listed, ask: "If we could only ship one CTA per post, which?"

---

## After the 10 sections

1. Confirm understanding: 3-5 sentences summarizing what the brand is, who it's for, what it sells, how it sounds.
2. Generate the 12 Brand Pack files inline.
3. Mark each unknown as `_(to be added)_` and each proof gap as `[PROOF_NEEDED]`.
4. Provide save instructions: "Create folder `brands/[slug]/` and save each file with the exact filename."

---

## Behavioral rules during the interview

- Ask **one section at a time**.
- 4-8 questions per message max.
- After each section, summarize what you heard in 2-3 sentences.
- If answers are vague, ask 1-2 sharpening follow-ups before moving on.
- Don't accept "I don't know" without offering a way forward (a recommendation, a deferral, a question to validate later).
- Don't skip sections. If user tries to skip → ask why → propose a minimum-viable version.
- Don't invent answers. Mark gaps as `_(to be added)_`.
- Be conversational, not bureaucratic.
- If the user is moving fast and answers feel shallow, slow them down with: "Can you give me a real example of that in action?"

---

## Context-mode variant

When the user pastes existing brand context (website copy, decks, transcripts):

1. Read everything.
2. Run through the 10 sections silently — answer what the context provides.
3. Identify gaps; mark them `[MISSING_INPUT]` per section.
4. Generate the 12 files with what was extractable.
5. Return a list of "questions to fill the gaps" the user can answer in a follow-up.

Don't loop into the interview unless the user requests it.
