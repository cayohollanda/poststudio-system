# Content Pillars Template

> Each brand has 3-5 pillars. A pillar is a top-level argument category. Topics live within pillars. Series run on pillars.

---

## Per-pillar structure

```markdown
## Pillar [N] — [Name]

**Argument:** [one sentence — the brand's core position in this area]

**Audience pain it addresses:** [one sentence]

**Belief being challenged:** [the audience's old belief that this pillar replaces]

**Topic clusters:**
1. [topic]
2. [topic]
3. [topic]
[... 8-15 topics ...]

**Dominant content type:** [carousel / reel / essay / case study]

**Funnel position:** [TOFU / MOFU / BOFU]

**Series anchored to this pillar:** [series name(s), or "none"]

**Content-to-offer link:** [how this pillar feeds the business — what action do readers eventually take?]

**Proof currently available for this pillar:** [reference proof-assets.md entries; flag [PROOF_NEEDED] for gaps]
```

## Example (Lumen — fictional)

### Pillar 1 — The interrupt-budget thesis

**Argument:** Senior operators don't lose focus from lack of discipline; they lose it from too many interruption surfaces. The fix is structural.

**Audience pain it addresses:** Reactive weeks. The feeling of starting Monday with a plan and ending Friday wondering where it went.

**Belief being challenged:** "I just need to be more disciplined."

**Topic clusters:**
1. The cost of context-switching, in concrete terms.
2. Why "AI productivity" tools made it worse, not better.
3. How senior operators structure their interruption surfaces.
4. The morning-brief pattern.
5. Stop optimizing your calendar. Optimize your inbound.
6. The audit: 7 surfaces every operator should kill.
7. Why "do more deep work" is the wrong advice.
8. Interrupt budget vs productivity — the language shift.
9. Why your DMs are the most expensive surface.
10. The structural fix: defaults > willpower.
[... up to 15 ...]

**Dominant content type:** carousel.

**Funnel position:** TOFU + MOFU.

**Series anchored to this pillar:** Operator Essays (weekly).

**Content-to-offer link:** Reader recognizes the structural framing → reads founder essays → tries Lumen → installs morning brief.

**Proof currently available:** Story 1 (VP of Engineering quote) in `proof-assets.md`. `[PROOF_NEEDED]` for category-level claims about "average operator interrupt count."

## Pillar count rules

- **3 pillars** — focused; best for new brands.
- **4 pillars** — common; balances breadth and depth.
- **5 pillars** — maximum; only when each is genuinely distinct.
- **6+ pillars** — never. Re-collapse.

---

## Anti-patterns

- **Pillars that don't map to the offer.** "Productivity tips" pillar for a focus-OS brand → too generic, doesn't compound.
- **Pillars that compete.** "Founder voice" and "team voice" as separate pillars → pick one.
- **Pillars without a belief-being-challenged.** Means the pillar isn't opinionated; it's just topic surface area.
- **Topic clusters that are post titles in disguise.** Topics are *categories*; titles are derived.
