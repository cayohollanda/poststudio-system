# B-Roll Directions

> How to direct B-roll cuts — concrete, observable, named-purpose. Not "B-roll of laptop."

---

## The rule

Every B-roll cut has:

- **Duration** in seconds.
- **Purpose** (what it shows / why it's there).
- **Visual specifics** (subject, action, framing).
- **Optional:** lighting, lens, motion.

---

## Format

```
B-roll Cut N (Xs): [purpose] — [visual specifics] — [optional notes]
```

### Example (Lumen reel, 30s)

```
B-roll Cut 1 (3s): tight on a paper calendar page being torn — symbol of a meeting being killed — shot on a wooden desk in warm dusk light.
B-roll Cut 2 (2s): wide of an empty desk at golden hour — protected morning recovered — atmospheric haze, no people in frame.
B-roll Cut 3 (3s): hands closing a laptop deliberately — end of work — only the hands visible, framed from behind.
B-roll Cut 4 (4s): single brass key resting on wooden desk — the move you save for later — shallow DOF, single warm rim light.
```

---

## Categories of B-roll

### Hands working
Specific verbs: typing, closing, opening, pouring, writing, signing, tapping. Don't say "hands working."

### Product close-up
Single object, hyperreal material detail, single key light. Use Brand Pack visual-style language.

### Environment
Wide shot of a workspace, room, street, exterior. Sets context.

### Before/after split
Same subject, two states, same lighting.

### Reference object metaphor
Object that *means* something for the post (key = unlock, dome = focus, calendar = time).

### Demonstration
Real action: someone using the product, completing a task, observing a result.

## Per-platform B-roll style

| Platform | Pace | Cuts per 30s |
|---|---|---|
| TikTok | fast | 6-10 |
| Instagram Reels | mid-fast | 5-8 |
| YouTube Shorts | mid | 4-7 |
| LinkedIn video | slower | 3-5 |
| X video | mid | 4-6 |

---

## Banned B-roll directions

- "B-roll of laptop" → too vague.
- "Stock footage of [scene]" → use real footage or rendered.
- "AI-generated person doing [thing]" → unless the brand allows AI people.
- "Logos of clients" → unless permission and Brand Pack-aligned.
- "Smiling stock-photo team" → almost always wrong.

---

## When the brand bans faces

Use:

- Hands-only shots (back-of-hand, partial frame).
- Back-of-head shots.
- Object-led shots (product, environment, metaphor).
- Silhouettes against light.

Never include faces if `constraints.md` bans them.

---

## B-roll → on-screen text alignment

Each B-roll cut should align with one on-screen text block. Rule of thumb:

```
Cut: [visual]
On-screen text: [≤6 words that match the visual's purpose]
```

If the on-screen text says "I tested 14 tools" and the cut is a wide shot of an empty desk, the audience is confused. Match cut to text.

---

## When NOT to use B-roll

- 15s scripts with strong talking-head delivery — let the founder carry it.
- Very fast pacing where 6+ cuts in 15s would feel chaotic.
- Tutorial scripts where one continuous demonstration shot is clearer.

Even when minimal, the first 2 seconds need a strong visual.
