# Scope Boundaries

> What's in scope, what's out of scope, and how to say no without losing the client.

---

## In scope (default content engagement)

- Brand Pack creation + quarterly refresh.
- Brand diagnosis on engagement start + on request.
- Content strategy: pillars, series, monthly plan.
- Carousel production.
- Reels script production.
- Multi-post campaigns.
- Visual direction + image prompts.
- Quality Gate / critique pass on every output.
- Monthly reporting.
- Renderable carousel output (if Mode 3 is part of the engagement).

---

## Out of scope (default)

These are NOT included unless explicitly contracted as add-ons:

- Image / video production (we direct; client renders or contracts a designer).
- Final post publishing (client owns the publish action and credentials).
- Paid ad creative or media buying.
- Email marketing copy.
- Long-form blog posts > 1500 words.
- Sales page / website copy.
- Press releases.
- Crisis comms.
- Direct customer interaction (DMs, comments responses).
- Influencer partnerships.
- Translation / localization beyond the agreed languages.
- Social media community management.
- Analytics setup or attribution work.

---

## How to handle out-of-scope requests

Don't say "no" sharply. Say:

> "That's out of scope for the current engagement. Here's how I'd suggest handling it: [option 1], [option 2]. If you want us to take it on, here's what it would look like to add: [scope, timeline, fee]."

Capture the request even if it's not taken on. Patterns of requests reveal what the next engagement should include.

---

## When the client expands scope informally

- 1-2 informal asks per month → absorb gracefully if they're small.
- Recurring patterns → name it: "we've handled X 4 times in 6 weeks; it's becoming a regular need. Should we formalize?"
- Sudden scope explosion → pause, schedule a re-scoping call.

---

## When the client wants to remove scope

- "We don't need a monthly plan anymore" → ask why; usually they need it more, not less.
- "We don't need quality reviews" → never agree. Quality Gate is non-negotiable.
- "We'll handle the visuals ourselves" → fine; deliver image prompts only and confirm.

---

## Scope change format

When scope changes, document in writing:

```
Scope change — YYYY-MM-DD

What's added: [...]
What's removed: [...]
Effective date: YYYY-MM-DD
Fee impact: [...]
```

Both parties acknowledge in writing.

---

## Scope discipline

A clear scope is the difference between an engagement that ships great work and one that drowns. Hold the line politely. Don't apologize for the boundary.
