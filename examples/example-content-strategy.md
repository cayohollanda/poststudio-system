# Example Content Strategy — `lumen` (fictional, May 2026)

> Worked example. 30-day strategy for Lumen, May 2026.

**Period:** 2026-05-01 to 2026-05-31
**Channel scope:** LinkedIn primary, Instagram secondary
**Cadence:** 4 posts/week LinkedIn, 2/week IG (cross-posted)

---

## Theme

Push the **interrupt-budget thesis** for the first 12 days, then lead a **14-day product launch** for v3.4 Focus Mode (May 13-26), then close the month with proof + reflection.

---

## Pillars (active this period)

### Pillar 1 — The interrupt-budget thesis (45% of content this month)
- Topic clusters this period: cost of context-switching, "AI productivity" tax, structural-not-behavioral, decision fatigue (NEW from diagnosis), the morning-brief pattern.
- Series anchored: Operator Essays (Wednesdays).

### Pillar 2 — Post-chat AI (25%)
- Topic clusters: chat is interruption with a friendly face, the case for removing the chat box, what comes after chat.
- Series anchored: Engineering Essays (alternate Fridays).

### Pillar 3 — Senior-operator playbook (20%)
- Topic clusters: 3 questions before adopting a tool, default behaviors > heroic behaviors, what senior operators don't engage with.
- Series anchored: shared with Pillar 1.

### Pillar 4 — Build-in-public (10%)
- Topic clusters: the no-chat decision, what 30 days of writing taught us, the day my brief was wrong.
- Series anchored: Engineering Essays.

---

## Recurring series (active)

- **Operator Essays** — weekly, Wednesday, carousel, Mira's voice, 7-9 slides, cinematic-dark.
- **Engineering Essays** — bi-weekly, Friday, carousel, Theo's voice, 8-10 slides, technical-blueprint or cinematic-dark.
- **Data Drop** — monthly, first Monday, carousel, brand voice, 9 slides with methodology slide.
- **We Said No** — quarterly. Not running this month.
