# Caption

I installed fourteen "AI productivity" tools last quarter. By the end of it, my deep-work time had been cut in half.

The tools weren't the problem. The interaction model was.

Every chat box is an interruption with a friendly face. You go in to do one thing, you come out three tabs deeper, and your morning has dissolved.

Senior operators aren't more disciplined. They have fewer surfaces where they *can* be interrupted. The advantage is structural, not behavioral.

What I removed first, in order:

1. Anything that lived in a chat sidebar of an existing tool.
2. Anything that pinged me before I'd finished my first read of the day.
3. Anything that asked me to "configure" rather than just running with sensible defaults.

What replaced them is a system that gives me one read at 8am, one read at 5pm, and nothing in between.

If your AI stack has gotten louder than your work, it's probably not the model. It's the interface.

Tell me — what's the one tool you'd kill in your stack tomorrow if you could?

— Mira
