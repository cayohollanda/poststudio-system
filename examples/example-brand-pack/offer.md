# Offer — `lumen`

> Lumen is the focus operating system for senior operators who've stopped believing chat-shaped AI can give them their week back.

---

## What we sell

A desktop and mobile app that runs in the background, ingests calendar + inbox + Slack signals, and produces:

1. A **morning brief** — what to focus on, what to defer, what to kill — delivered as a single 90-second read at a user-chosen time.
2. **Two protected deep-work blocks per day** — Lumen automatically declines ad-hoc meetings during these blocks, with a context-aware response that the user previewed once.
3. An **end-of-day reset** — a 60-second "what shipped, what didn't, what's tomorrow's brief" generated from the day's signal.

There is no chat interface. There is no sidebar assistant. There is no "talk to your data."

## The job to be done

> "I need to start every morning knowing exactly what I'm working on, and I need that decision to survive the next 4 hours, without me having to defend it 12 times."

## The unique mechanism

Most AI tools demand attention to *give* you leverage. Lumen *takes* attention to give you leverage.

The mechanism is *interruption budget management*. Lumen tracks the user's interruption surface area (number of meetings, DMs, ad-hoc requests) and automatically prunes it on their behalf — declining, deferring, or summarizing inbound work into the morning brief. The user does not write a single prompt.

This works because the senior-operator advantage is structural, not behavioral: the best operators are not "more focused"; they have *fewer surfaces where they can be interrupted.* Lumen replicates that structure.

## Core use cases

1. **The locked morning brief** — Lumen runs at the user's chosen time (typically 8am) and produces a single page: 3 priorities, 2 declines, 1 kill. The user reads it once, accepts it, and moves on. No re-decision throughout the day.
2. **Auto-defended deep work** — Two 90-minute blocks per day where ad-hoc meetings, DMs, and notifications are auto-handled. Real urgents (defined by the user) are surfaced; everything else is summarized at the end of the block.
3. **The 60-second daily reset** — At end-of-day, Lumen produces a sub-1-minute summary: what shipped, what didn't, what tomorrow's brief should weigh.

## Use cases we explicitly *don't* serve

- **Generic Q&A.** We don't answer "what's the weather?" or "summarize this PDF." Use a chat tool for that.
- **Real-time meeting assistance.** We do not transcribe meetings, generate live notes, or coach you in calls.
- **Inbox-zero ambitions.** We don't help you reach inbox-zero. Inbox-zero is a coping mechanism. We help you *stop* needing inbox-zero.
- **Team-wide rollouts to non-operators.** Lumen is for individuals making interruption decisions for themselves. We don't sell a "team dashboard."

> Saying no to the team-dashboard market is the most expensive product decision Lumen has made — and we'll keep saying it.

## Differentiators (3-5)

1. **No chat interface, by design.** Every other "AI productivity tool" puts a chat box at the center. Lumen has none. The mechanism is the absence.
2. **Auto-defense, not auto-suggestion.** Most tools suggest you protect deep work. Lumen *protects* it without re-asking you.
3. **One read per day, by default.** The morning brief and the end-of-day reset are the only required interactions. Everything else runs without you.
4. **Privacy-first architecture.** Calendar/inbox processing happens on-device when possible; cloud calls are scoped, ephemeral, and explicitly not used for training. (Per `proof-assets.md`.)
5. **Operator-grade defaults.** The product opinionatedly mirrors how senior operators actually run their week — pre-baked, not pre-configured.

## Alternatives the audience considers

### Reclaim / Clockwise (auto-scheduling)
- **Type:** direct adjacent (calendar AI).
- **Where they win:** team-coordinated calendars; "smart" 1:1 scheduling.
- **Where we win:** the underlying decision load. Reclaim shuffles your calendar; Lumen *prunes* it. They are calendar-managers; we are operating-posture tools.

### General-purpose AI chat tools
- **Type:** indirect; current default.
- **Where they win:** open-ended Q&A, drafting, exploration.
- **Where we win:** anything that requires sustained focus. Their chat-shaped interaction model is, by design, an interruption.

### Notion / Obsidian / second-brain stacks
- **Type:** adjacent; capture and organization.
- **Where they win:** long-term knowledge management, async docs.
- **Where we win:** execution. Capture without execution is a reading habit. Lumen handles the "what now."

### "Do nothing" — the calendar of one's company
- **Why audiences default here:** "I'll be more disciplined next week." It's free, it requires no install, it preserves the illusion of control.
- **What it costs them:** measurably, two reactive days per week. Anecdotally, the entire week.
