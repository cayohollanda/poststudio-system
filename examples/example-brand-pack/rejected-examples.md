# Rejected Examples — `lumen`

> Patterns banned because they hurt the brand. Lumen is fictional; entries below are illustrative.

---

## Banned patterns

### Pattern: Listicle hook ("5 ways...")
- **Description:** Hooks structured as numbered lists.
- **Example:** "5 ways AI is changing work." (carousel, 2026-01-30)
- **Why it failed:** Generic hook, no audience-specific framing, no mechanism. Could have been from any AI brand. 19 saves, 3 comments — far below average.
- **What to do instead:** Confession + specific number ("I installed 14 AI tools last quarter") OR named-pattern hook ("the meeting that should have been a doc").
- **Date banned:** 2026-02-15.
- **Banned by:** Mira after monthly retro.

### Pattern: Direct competitor comparison
- **Description:** Naming a real competitor in a side-by-side comparison.
- **Example:** "Lumen vs Reclaim" carousel (2026-02-08).
- **Why it failed:** Audience read as adversarial. Comments split between defending Reclaim and questioning whether we were ready to be in a "vs" post. Compliance flagged it post-hoc.
- **What to do instead:** Compete on positioning at the category level ("calendar-AI tools," "chat-shaped AI"). Never name competitors.
- **Date banned:** 2026-02-12.
- **Banned by:** legal review.

### Pattern: All-caps emphasis
- **Description:** "THE ONE THING..." / "I CANNOT BELIEVE..."
- **Why it failed:** Doesn't fit the quiet-confident voice. Reads as spam.
- **What to do instead:** Restrained emphasis through accent color on a single word. The voice is operator-direct, not LinkedIn-bro.
- **Date banned:** 2026-02-19.

### Pattern: Three-line poetry layout
- **Description:** "this. is. a. line." caption formatting where each phrase is a separate line.
- **Why it failed:** Contradicts our restrained voice. Reads as theatrical.
- **What to do instead:** Conversational paragraphs with one longer sentence per paragraph for rhythm.
- **Date banned:** 2026-02-19.

### Pattern: Mid-post product plug
- **Description:** Mentioning Lumen by name in the middle of a founder essay.
- **Example:** A 2026-03-04 carousel pivoted to "Lumen does this" on slide 4.
- **Why it failed:** Broke trust. Audience reaction was "this turned into an ad mid-essay."
- **What to do instead:** Reserve product mentions for slide 5+ in product-led structure only. In founder essays, no product mention at all (or only in the final slide if natural).
- **Date banned:** 2026-03-08.

---

## Hook patterns banned

- "Are you tired of...?"
- "Did you know that...?"
- "Hot take:" / "Unpopular opinion:"
- "Buckle up." / "Strap in."
- All listicle hooks ("5 ways...", "7 things...").
- Engagement bait ("Drop a 🔥 if you agree").

---

## Visual patterns banned

- Bright daytime imagery. Lumen's light is evening or pre-dawn.
- Two-accent gradients. We use one amber accent only.
- Real product UI screenshots on slide 1. Reads as SaaS landing page.
- Faces of any kind (real or AI-generated). Off-brand entirely.
- Stock-photo composition. Always cinematic / editorial / surreal-product-metaphor.

---

## Caption / CTA patterns banned

- "Hope this helps!"
- "Let me know your thoughts."
- "Thoughts?"
- "Tag a friend who needs this" (engagement bait).
- Hashtag soup at the end (3 max, brand-pack approved only).
- "Drop a 🔥" / "Drop a 💯" emoji-reactions.

---

## Patterns we tried that almost worked

### Almost: "Lumen as your morning chief of staff"
- **What we tried:** A founder essay framing Lumen as "the chief of staff for senior operators."
- **Why it didn't quite land:** Audience associated "chief of staff" with hiring a person. The metaphor pushed the wrong category association.
- **What might fix it:** Different framing — "the operating posture of a senior operator, externalized." More structural, less personnel.
- **Status:** parked. Revisit if positioning shifts.

---

## Banned for compliance reasons

- "FDA-approved" — never (we are not FDA-approved).
- "Scientifically proven" — never (research-grounded language only with citations).
- "100% private" — never (use specific privacy facts: "on-device processing," "no training on your data").
- Specific health-outcome claims — never. Lumen is not a clinical product.
