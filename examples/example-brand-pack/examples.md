# Examples — `lumen`

> What's worked for Lumen, what hasn't, and the patterns the system should lean on. Illustrative — Lumen is fictional.

---

## What's worked

### Worked 1 — "I installed 14 AI tools in a quarter. My deep work collapsed."
- **Topic / hook:** Mira's confession of over-installing AI tools and watching her deep-work time get cut in half.
- **Format:** carousel (8 slides), founder-perspective + mistake angle.
- **Platform:** LinkedIn.
- **Date:** 2026-02-19.
- **Why it landed:** the hook combines a specific number (14) with a confession, lowering the audience's defenses. The slide-2 "what I removed first" promise turned the post into a workflow reveal. The voice was unmistakably Mira's — peer-talking-to-peer, not founder-pitching.
- **Metric:** 412 saves, 87 comments, 14 inbound trials. Most-saved Lumen post to date.
- **What to repeat:** confession + specific number; founder-voice + workflow-reveal; cinematic-dark visual mode.

### Worked 2 — "The meeting that should have been a doc"
- **Topic / hook:** named the universal experience of a 90-minute meeting that resolved one decision.
- **Format:** carousel (7 slides), contrarian + mechanism angle.
- **Platform:** LinkedIn.
- **Date:** 2026-03-04.
- **Why it landed:** named a feeling the audience had but didn't have language for. The phrase "the meeting that should have been a doc" became audience-adopted vocabulary.
- **Metric:** 287 saves, 142 comments, 9 inbound trials.
- **What to repeat:** name the unspoken pattern; give the audience a phrase they can reuse.

### Worked 3 — "Stop installing AI tools that interrupt you"
- **Topic / hook:** prescriptive frame against the install-everything default.
- **Format:** carousel (8 slides), "Stop Doing X" angle.
- **Platform:** LinkedIn + Instagram.
- **Date:** 2026-03-21.
- **Why it landed:** strong directive hook + slide 4 mechanism (the structural-not-behavioral argument). The CTA "save for the next time you're about to install another AI extension" matched the post energy.
- **Metric:** 318 saves, 64 comments, 11 inbound trials.
- **What to repeat:** prescriptive hook + structural mechanism + matched CTA energy.

### Worked 4 — "How I lost my mornings to AI in three weeks"
- **Topic / hook:** Mira tracks the exact week-by-week deterioration of her mornings as she added tools.
- **Format:** carousel (9 slides), Mistake + Workflow Reveal.
- **Platform:** LinkedIn.
- **Date:** 2026-04-02.
- **Why it landed:** specific timeline, named tools by category (not by brand), ended on the Lumen morning-brief replacement. Felt like a documentary, not an ad.
- **Metric:** 256 saves, 71 comments, 19 inbound trials.
- **What to repeat:** week-by-week storytelling; documentary feel; product reveal in the final slides only.

### Worked 5 — "We removed the chat box and shipped"
- **Topic / hook:** Theo's engineering essay on the design decision to ship without a chat interface.
- **Format:** carousel (8 slides), Founder + Build in Public.
- **Platform:** LinkedIn + X.
- **Date:** 2026-04-15.
- **Why it landed:** unusual decision, candid framing, technical readers loved the build-in-public angle. The "no chat" positioning got reinforced.
- **Metric:** 198 saves, 53 comments, 7 inbound trials.
- **What to repeat:** Theo's engineering voice for technical-leadership audiences; product decisions framed as opinionated essays.

## What didn't work

### Didn't 1 — "5 ways AI is changing work"
- **Topic / hook:** generic listicle on AI and work culture.
- **Format:** carousel (8 slides), no specific angle.
- **Platform:** LinkedIn.
- **Date:** 2026-01-30.
- **Why it underperformed:** generic hook, no audience-specific framing, no mechanism slide. Could have been from any AI brand.
- **Metric:** 19 saves, 3 comments. Below average.
- **Lesson:** never ship a listicle without an opinionated edge. Banned.

### Didn't 2 — "Lumen vs Reclaim"
- **Topic / hook:** direct comparison with Reclaim.
- **Format:** carousel (7 slides), Comparison angle.
- **Platform:** LinkedIn.
- **Date:** 2026-02-08.
- **Why it underperformed:** audience read it as adversarial. Comments split between defending Reclaim and questioning whether we were ready to be in a "vs" post. Compliance also flagged it post-hoc — see constraints.md.
- **Metric:** 11 saves, 28 comments (mostly defensive).
- **Lesson:** never name competitors directly. Compete on positioning, not on side-by-sides. Banned.

### Didn't 3 — "How to be more productive with AI"
- **Topic / hook:** pure educational framing.
- **Format:** carousel (10 slides), educational angle.
- **Platform:** LinkedIn.
- **Date:** 2026-02-25.
- **Why it underperformed:** too broad; tried to teach the whole topic; no tension; no specific audience. Looks like every other "AI productivity" post.
- **Metric:** 14 saves, 6 comments.
- **Lesson:** narrow the topic; pick a specific audience moment; lead with tension, not "tips."

## Banned patterns (we tried these, they hurt the brand)

- **Listicle hooks ("5 ways," "7 things").** Reason: underperformed and made the brand look generic. Banned.
- **Direct competitor comparisons by name.** Reason: read adversarial; compliance flag. Banned.
- **All-caps emphasis ("The ONE thing...").** Reason: doesn't fit the quiet-confident voice. Banned.
- **Three-line poetry layouts in captions** ("this. is. a. line."). Reason: contradicts our "restrained, not theatrical" voice. Banned.
- **Mid-post product plugs.** Reason: broke trust on a Worked-1-quality founder post. Product mentions reserved for slides 5+ in product-led structure only.

## Rituals (recurring content patterns we've kept)

- **Weekly: Mira's "operator essay"** — Wednesday morning. Founder-voice carousel on a single operator-decision-making topic.
- **Bi-weekly: Theo's "engineering essay"** — every other Friday. Build-in-public or technical-decision essay for engineering-leadership audiences. Carousel + cross-posted to X.
- **Monthly: a "data drop"** — the first Monday of each month. Benchmark or proof-led carousel using a metric from `proof-assets.md`. Includes methodology slide.
- **Quarterly: a "we said no" post** — every quarter, an essay on a request we've turned down. Builds positioning by negation.

## Hook patterns the audience responds to

- **Confession + specific number** — "I installed 14 AI tools..."
- **Named pattern** — "The meeting that should have been a doc."
- **Prescriptive directive** — "Stop installing AI tools that..."
- **Mechanism callout** — "The senior-operator advantage is structural, not behavioral."
- **Documentary timeline** — "How I lost my mornings in three weeks."

## Hook patterns that don't work for our audience

- **Generic listicles** ("5 things..."). Banned.
- **"Hot take:"** openers. Banned.
- **All-caps urgency** ("THE ONE THING..."). Banned.
- **Question-as-hook with no specificity** ("Are you struggling with focus?"). Banned.
- **Engagement bait** ("Drop a 🔥 if you agree"). Banned.

---

## Visual patterns the audience responds to

- **Cinematic Dark + warm amber accent + serif headline + bottom-third typography.** Highest thumbnail-stop rate.
- **Surreal product metaphor for slide 1, switching to Cinematic Dark for slides 2-N.** Used in Worked 1 and Worked 4. Strong.
- **Restrained typography (no decorative fonts, no gradient text, no shadows).** Reads as confident.

## Visual patterns that don't work

- **Bright daytime imagery.** Tried twice. Underperformed both times. Off-brand.
- **Two-accent palettes.** Read cluttered at thumbnail.
- **Real product UI screenshots on slide 1.** Audience scrolled past — looked like a SaaS landing page.
- **People / faces.** Off-brand entirely. Banned in `constraints.md`.

---

## Captions / CTA patterns we want to preserve

### Caption opener patterns that worked
- **Restate the hook in different words on line 1.** Then the strongest sentence from the carousel on line 2-3.
- **Confession in the first sentence, no preamble.**

### Caption closing / CTA patterns that worked
- **One question targeting a specific decision the audience is making.** "What's the one tool you'd kill in your stack tomorrow?"
- **"Save this for the next time you're about to..."** when the carousel is utility-heavy.
- **"DM me '[KEYWORD]' and I'll send the [bonus]."** for high-intent narrow audiences.

### CTAs that flopped
- "Thoughts?" — no specific action, low-effort.
- "What do you think?" — same problem.
- "Drop a 🔥 if you agree" — felt low-effort and reduced our save rate.
