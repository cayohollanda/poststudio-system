# Competitors — `lumen`

> ⚠️ Lumen is fictional; competitor names below are illustrative categories, not real brands. We refer to alternatives by category, never by name in posts.

---

## Direct competitors

### Calendar-AI category (e.g. category leaders we don't name in posts)

- **Type:** direct adjacent.
- **What they do:** auto-schedule calendar blocks, smart 1:1 scheduling, focus-time defense.
- **Where they win:** team-coordinated calendars; smart 1:1 placement; multi-person scheduling.
- **Where we win:** the underlying decision load. They shuffle calendar; Lumen prunes it. They are calendar-managers; we are operating-posture tools.
- **Audience overlap:** mid-high.
- **Off-limits to name in posts:** yes (per `constraints.md`).
- **Last reviewed:** 2026-04-15.

### General-purpose AI chat tools

- **Type:** indirect; the current default.
- **What they do:** open-ended Q&A, drafting, exploration.
- **Where they win:** one-off tasks, research, drafting, brainstorming.
- **Where we win:** anything requiring sustained focus. Their chat-shaped interaction model is, by design, an interruption.
- **Audience overlap:** universal.
- **Off-limits to name in posts:** yes.
- **Last reviewed:** 2026-04-15.

## Adjacent products

### Notion / Obsidian / Roam (second-brain category)

- **Type:** adjacent; capture and organization.
- **Where they win:** long-term knowledge management, async docs, team wiki.
- **Where we win:** execution. Capture without execution is a reading habit. Lumen handles "what now."
- **When the audience picks them over us:** when knowledge structuring is the actual problem (vs interrupt budget).

### Pomodoro / focus timer apps

- **Type:** adjacent; behavioral focus tools.
- **Where they win:** simple, free, no setup.
- **Where we win:** structural fix vs behavioral hack. Our thesis is timers don't fix the underlying surface count.

---

## Internal hacks / DIY

### "I'll just be more disciplined this week"

- **What it is:** willpower.
- **Why audiences use it:** free; preserves the illusion of control.
- **What we offer over it:** structural defaults that don't require willpower. Discipline isn't compounding; structure is.

### Custom Notion + calendar-blocking SOP

- **What it is:** a personal SOP using Notion + manual calendar blocks.
- **Why audiences use it:** they have one already.
- **What we offer over it:** the auto-defense layer (Lumen *enforces* without re-asking). Their SOP fails by week 3 of the year.

---

## "Do nothing" — the most important competitor

- **Why audiences default here:** "I'll be more disciplined next week." It's free, it requires no install, it preserves the illusion of control.
- **What it costs them:** measurably, two reactive days per week. Anecdotally, the entire week.
- **The trigger that makes them act:** typically a Friday afternoon where the week ended with nothing shipped despite a packed schedule. Or 6 weeks of mornings that "didn't survive."
- **How we surface this:** founder-confessional carousels (Mira's "14 tools in a quarter" pattern).
