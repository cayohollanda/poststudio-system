# Voice — `lumen`

> Quietly confident. Operator-direct. Slightly nocturnal. Treats the reader like a peer who's already tired of being sold to.

---

## Voice in 6 adjectives

1. **Operator-direct** — written like a senior practitioner, not a marketer.
2. **Quietly confident** — takes positions plainly without yelling.
3. **Slightly nocturnal** — feels like a 9pm desk, not a midday office.
4. **Dryly funny** — humor exists, but never wags its tail.
5. **Specific** — uses concrete numbers, named patterns, real situations.
6. **Restrained** — chooses one strong sentence over three medium ones.

## Register

- **Formality:** informal-direct
- **Person:** first-singular ("I") for Mira's posts; first-plural ("we") for company posts; never third-person.
- **Lowercase-friendly:** yes — captions and supporting copy default to sentence case; headlines on slides are sentence case.
- **Emoji policy:** none in body copy. One small accent emoji allowed in captions only when it's actually load-bearing (rare). Never in headlines.
- **Sentence length:** mostly short, with one longer sentence per paragraph for rhythm. Mix.
- **Punctuation:** Oxford comma. Em dashes used sparingly (≤1 per paragraph). No semicolons in social copy. No exclamation marks except in dialogue or the rare moment of genuine surprise.

## What the voice *does*

- Names the reader's exact situation in 1-2 sentences before introducing the brand.
- Uses concrete numbers when available; marks `[PROOF_NEEDED]` when not.
- Argues from mechanism, not from outcome alone.
- Admits the brand's limits ("we don't do X") openly.
- Uses domain-specific vocabulary that the audience uses ("interrupt budget," "context-switch tax").
- Closes with a question that invites a real answer, not "thoughts?"
- Treats the reader as a peer making the same decisions Mira/Theo make.

## What the voice *does not* do

- LinkedIn-bro openers: "Here's the thing." / "Hot take:" / "Let me explain."
- Hype intensifiers: "incredible," "insane," "absolutely."
- Hashtag soup at the end of captions (one hashtag max, only when relevant).
- Three-line poetry layout. No "this. is. a. line."
- "Friends," / "Fam," / "Hey founders!" openers.
- Comparing Lumen to other named products inside posts.
- Ending captions with "Hope this helps!" or "Thoughts?"

## Words to avoid (banned)

### Marketing-jargon banlist (default — strictly enforced)
- "transform" / "transformative"
- "supercharge"
- "unleash" / "unlock" (when used as marketing verbs)
- "synergy" / "synergize"
- "leverage" (as a verb; allowed as a noun: "leverage point")
- "robust"
- "seamless"
- "world-class"
- "industry-leading"
- "best-in-class"
- "game-changer" / "game-changing"
- "innovative"
- "cutting-edge"
- "10x" (overused; only allowed in audience quotes)
- "AI-powered" (we use "the model decides," "the brief is generated by," "Lumen reads")

### Filler-intensifier banlist
- "absolutely"
- "literally"
- "100%"
- "truly"
- "really" (most cases)
- "very" (most cases)
- "actually" (in promotional contexts)

### Stale-pattern banlist
- "Are you tired of..."
- "Did you know that..."
- "Hot take:"
- "Unpopular opinion:"
- "Buckle up." / "Strap in."
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "Imagine a world where..."
- "Here's the thing..."
- "Let me explain..."
- "Hope this helps!"
- "Let me know your thoughts."
- "Drop a 🔥 if you agree"
- "Friends," / "Fam,"

### Brand-specific banlist
- "AI assistant" — Lumen is not an assistant.
- "chatbot" — pejorative in our mouth.
- "all-in-one" — we are explicitly not.
- "easy" / "effortless" — we don't claim ease; we claim *fewer decisions*.
- "boost productivity" — we don't measure productivity; we measure interrupt budget.

## Voice examples (gold standards)

### Example 1 — short post
> Most "AI productivity tools" are chat windows pretending to be assistants. They interrupt your day to give you leverage. We do the opposite: Lumen takes attention to give you back the morning.

### Example 2 — opener
> Most senior PMs lost their week the day someone added "AI" to their tab bar.

### Example 3 — caption closing
> If your AI stack has gotten louder than your work, it's not the model. It's the interface. Tell me — what's the one tool you'd kill in your stack tomorrow if you could?

### Example 4 — founder line
> I installed fourteen "AI productivity" tools in a quarter. By the end of it, my deep-work time had been cut in half. The tools weren't the problem. The interaction model was. We built Lumen because nobody else would.

## Voice anti-examples (bad)

### Anti-example 1
> "🚀 Tired of being interrupted? Lumen is the AI-powered productivity tool that transforms your workflow! Boost your focus by 10x! Try it free today!"
- Why it's wrong: marketing-jargon stack, intensifiers, exclamation marks, banned vocabulary, no specificity, no respect for the reader.

### Anti-example 2
> "Hey friends! Hot take: most AI tools are actually making you LESS productive. Here's the thing — you need a SYSTEM, not another chatbot. 🔥"
- Why it's wrong: stale opener, all-caps, "Here's the thing," emoji, opinion-without-mechanism, "friends" hello.

### Anti-example 3
> "Lumen is best-in-class for senior operators. Our innovative, AI-powered platform leverages cutting-edge models to seamlessly transform your morning routine."
- Why it's wrong: pure jargon stack with no real claim. Every banned word in one sentence.

## Voice review checklist

Before shipping any caption or slide, run this:

1. **Read it out loud.** If you stumble, rewrite.
2. **Search for words from the banlist.** If you find any, rewrite.
3. **Check sentence rhythm.** No 4+ same-length sentences in a row.
4. **Compare to voice examples above.** Same brand?
5. **Mira test:** would Mira say this in a one-on-one with another senior operator? If no, rewrite.
