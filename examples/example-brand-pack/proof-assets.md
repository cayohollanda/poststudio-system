## Customer stories / case studies

### Story 1 — VP of Engineering at a Series-C devtools company
- **Customer:** anonymized (under standard NDA) — referred to publicly as "VP of Engineering at a Series-C devtools company."
- **Industry / segment:** developer tooling, ~280 employees.
- **Their problem before us:** their week had compressed into back-to-back 30-minute meetings; they had not held two consecutive hours of focused engineering work in nine weeks.
- **What we did:** during the first month on Lumen, they let the morning brief pick their priorities and turned on the auto-defended deep-work blocks (8:30-10:00 and 13:30-15:00).
- **Outcome:** by week 4, they reported four uninterrupted morning blocks per week and one full deep-work afternoon. They noted: "the brief stopped me from re-deciding the day at every Slack ping."
- **Permission to cite publicly:** yes-but-anonymized.
- **Quote (verbatim):** "The brief stopped me from re-deciding the day at every Slack ping." — VP of Engineering, Series-C devtools (used with permission, anonymized).
- **Last reviewed:** 2026-04-12

## Founder / operator anecdotes

### Anecdote 1 — Mira on the day she stopped using AI chat tools
> "I was in the middle of writing the spec for what would become Lumen. I had three AI chat tabs open. Every time I switched to one, I forgot the sentence I was on. By 11am I'd written nothing. That's the day I closed all three. The first version of Lumen was just a recurring 8am email I sent myself."

### Anecdote 2 — Theo on the build decision to remove the chat box
> "Our designer drafted a chat sidebar in the v0.3 mock. I asked him to delete it. He pushed back. We spent two weeks on whether to ship without one. We shipped without. Three months later, that decision is the entire brand."

### Anecdote 3 — Mira on the week the brief survived
> "I had the worst Tuesday of my year — three customer escalations before 10am. The brief said 'kill the meeting at 14:00.' I almost didn't. I did. That was the first afternoon in months I shipped a real strategy doc. The brief was right. I was wrong."

## Things the audience seemed to *care* about

- **The "no chat by design" positioning** — generated more comments and saves than any other angle.
- **Mira's confessional voice** — the "fourteen tools in a quarter" line has been quoted back to us by 11+ different operators.
- **The interrupt-budget framing** — vocabulary the audience adopted; we now see it in their own posts.
- **The Mode-1 (Cinematic Dark) visual** — outperforms our (rare) bright/editorial slides at thumbnail-stop rate.

## Things the audience seemed *not* to care about

- **Listicle posts ("5 things to do at 8am")** — underperformed our average. Banned.
- **Direct comparison posts vs Reclaim/Clockwise** — read as adversarial; audience didn't want a category battle.
- **Behind-the-scenes "team building" posts** — felt off-brand for the operator audience.
