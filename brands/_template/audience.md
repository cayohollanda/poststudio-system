# Audience — `[brand-slug]`

> Who the brand is for. The more specific you make this, the sharper every post will be. Vague audiences produce vague posts.

---

## Primary audience

- **Role:** [the job title or category — be specific. "Series A SaaS founders" beats "founders." "Senior PMs at 50-500 person SaaS" beats "PMs."]
- **Situation:** [the specific moment they're in — e.g. "just raised a Series A and are hiring their first 10 engineers."]
- **Where they are in the buying journey:** [unaware / problem-aware / solution-aware / product-aware / decision-stage]

### Their #1 pain
[One sentence in their own words, not yours. If you've never heard them say it out loud, mark it `_(to be validated)_` and prioritize talking to 5 people.]

### Their #1 desire
[One sentence in their own words. The outcome they'd happily pay for if it worked.]

### What they tried before
[2-4 alternatives they've already tested or considered, and why those didn't stick.]

### Beliefs they hold that are *getting in their way*
[2-3 beliefs that we'll need to dislodge to win them over. These are gold for "Contrarian" and "Myth vs Reality" angle posts.]

### Beliefs they hold that we *agree with*
[2-3 beliefs we share. These are anchors for "Founder Perspective" and "Build in Public" angle posts.]

### Where they hang out online
[2-4 specific places. "LinkedIn" is too vague. "LinkedIn comment threads under [SPECIFIC_PERSON_OR_PUBLICATION]" is useful.]

### What they read / watch / listen to
[2-5 specific publications, newsletters, podcasts, YouTube channels. This calibrates voice.]

## How they speak

- **Vocabulary they actually use:** [3-5 phrases or words. Capture verbatim from real conversations or comments.]
- **Vocabulary they hate:** [3-5 words/phrases that read as marketing-speak or insulting in their world.]
- **Reading level / formality:** [casual / mid / formal / mixed]
- **Lowercase or proper case in their own writing:** [observe, don't guess]
- **Are they ironic / earnest / both?**
