# Voice — `weaura-ai`

> Bilingual (PT-BR + EN), technical, anti-jargon, slightly playful. Senior platform engineer talking to senior platform engineer. Italics for emphasis (the "_conhece_" / "_knows_" pattern). No marketing fog.

---

## Voice in 6 adjectives

1. **Technical** — comfortable with kubectl, PromQL, Helm, OOMKilled. No watering-down.
2. **Anti-jargon** — refuses marketing speak. "AI-powered" is banned.
3. **Confident-but-not-loud** — takes positions plainly. Doesn't yell.
4. **Slightly playful** — italicized emphasis ("_conhece_"), dry contrasts ("a IA que adivinha vs a IA que _sabe_").
5. **Bilingual** — Portuguese (Brazilian) and English. Same voice in both.
6. **Operator-grade** — written like a senior practitioner, not a content marketer.

## Register

- **Formality:** mid-formal. Comfortable with technical terms; allergic to fluff.
- **Person:** first-plural ("we") for company posts. First-singular ("I") for the rare founder essay (when founders are public).
- **Lowercase-friendly:** yes — captions and supporting copy default to sentence case.
- **Emoji policy:** none in body copy. Emoji feels off-brand for this audience and vertical.
- **Sentence length:** short, punchy. One longer sentence per paragraph for rhythm.
- **Punctuation:** Oxford comma. Em dashes used sparingly. **Italics for emphasis** — particularly for the brand-signature pattern (one verb in italics that flips the meaning of a contrast).
- **Bilingual code-switching:** allowed within a single post when both languages add weight (e.g., "*A IA que **conhece** sua infraestrutura* — the AI that **knows** your infrastructure"). Don't translate every line. Use the language that lands hardest.

## What the voice *does*

- Names the audience's exact situation in 1-2 sentences ("seu pod está reiniciando há 4 horas; o RCA mora em 5 dashboards").
- Cites sources / numbers when available; uses `[PROOF_NEEDED]` placeholders when not.
- Argues from mechanism, not from outcome alone. ("Why" beats "wow.")
- Admits limits openly ("WeAura não faz remediação automática. Por design.").
- Uses domain vocabulary that the audience uses ("OOMKilled," "blast radius," "deploy timeline," "post-mortem drift").
- Uses italicized contrast as a signature ("uma IA que adivinha vs uma IA que _sabe_").
- Closes with a question that invites a real reply, not "Thoughts?"
- Treats the reader as a peer engineer.

## What the voice *does not* do

- LinkedIn-bro openers: "Here's the thing." / "Hot take:" / "Let me explain."
- Hype intensifiers: "incredible," "insane," "absolutely," "10x."
- Hashtag soup at the end of captions (1-2 hashtags max, only if relevant).
- Three-line poetry layout. No "this. is. a. line."
- "Friends," / "Fam," / "Hey engineers!" openers.
- Comparing WeAura to other named AI tools inside posts (compete on category positioning, not name-drops).
- Ending captions with "Hope this helps!" or "Thoughts?"
- Generic "AI-powered" framing.

- "supercharge"
- "unleash" / "unlock" (when used as marketing verbs)
- "synergy" / "synergize"
- "leverage" (as a verb)
- "robust"
- "seamless"

- "industry-leading"
- "best-in-class"
- "game-changer" / "game-changing"
- "innovative"
- "cutting-edge"
- "10x" / "100x" (overused)
- "next-gen"
- "revolutionary"
- "disrupt" / "disruption" / "disruptive"

### Filler-intensifier banlist
- "absolutely"
- "literally"
- "100%"
- "truly"
- "really" (most cases)

### Stale-pattern banlist
- "Are you tired of..."
- "Did you know that..."
- "Hot take:"
- "Unpopular opinion:"
- "Buckle up." / "Strap in."
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "Imagine a world where..."
- "Here's the thing..."
- "Let me explain..."
- "Hope this helps!"
- "Let me know your thoughts."
- "Drop a 🔥 if you agree"

### Brand-specific banlist
- **"AI-powered"** — generic; replace with the specific mechanism ("grounded in your infra," "with citation contract," "with HITL approval").
- **"AI assistant"** — too generic; we are a *grounded ops platform*, not an assistant.
- **"chatbot"** — wrong category; we are not chat-shaped over generic data.
- **"easy" / "effortless"** — we don't claim ease; we claim *fewer dashboards*.
- **"copilot"** — wrong category metaphor. We are not a coding copilot.

## Voice examples (gold standards)

### Example 1 — short post (PT)
> A maioria dos times de ops perde 4 horas por semana pivotando entre cinco dashboards. O bottleneck nunca foi o gráfico. Foi a correlação. *A IA que **conhece** sua infraestrutura* responde em 1.4s, com a fonte citada.

### Example 2 — short post (EN)
> Most ops teams lose 4 hours a week pivoting through five dashboards. The bottleneck was never the chart. It was the correlation. *The AI that **knows** your infrastructure* answers in 1.4s — with the source cited.

### Example 3 — opener
> Generic AI doesn't know why your `payment-api` is restarting. It can't. It has no context.

### Example 4 — caption closing
> WeAura returns the metric, the deploy SHA, the runbook line — every time. Citation contract is the product. — What's the longest RCA you've shipped in the last quarter? I'm collecting horror stories.

### Example 5 — founder line *(template — assign once founder is public)*
> Construímos o WeAura porque a gente cansou de descobrir o root cause na quinta tab. *Pare de adivinhar. **Saiba**.*

## Voice anti-examples (bad)

### Anti-example 1
> "🚀 Tired of slow incident response? WeAura is the AI-powered, next-gen platform that supercharges your SRE team! Boost productivity by 10x! Try it free today!"
- Why it's wrong: every banned word in one line. No specificity. No respect for the reader.

### Anti-example 2
> "Hey engineers! Hot take: most AI tools are actually making your incidents WORSE. Here's the thing — you need a SYSTEM, not another chatbot. 🔥"
- Why it's wrong: stale opener, all-caps, "Here's the thing," emoji, opinion-without-mechanism.

### Anti-example 3
> "WeAura is best-in-class for enterprise observability. Our innovative AI-powered platform leverages cutting-edge models to seamlessly transform your incident response."
- Why it's wrong: pure marketing fog. Every banned word in one paragraph.

## Voice review checklist

Before shipping any caption or slide, run this:

1. **Read it out loud.** If you stumble, rewrite.
2. **Search for words from the banlist.** If found, rewrite.
3. **Check sentence rhythm.** No 4+ same-length sentences in a row.
4. **Compare to voice examples above.** Same brand?
5. **Italicized-contrast test:** is there at least one sentence using the italicized-verb pattern when the post argues a contrast? (e.g. "_knows_" / "_saiba_" / "_grounded_")
6. **Senior-engineer test:** would a senior platform engineer read this and find the technical claim defensible? If no, rewrite.
